About Feeling Arrow

Feeling Arrow is a unique immersive archery game that uses carefully designed control mechanisms to let players realistically experience the entire process of drawing, aiming, and shooting arrows. The game emphasizes not just accuracy in hitting targets, but also the fluidity and authenticity of the archery experience. In the game, players need to master arrow power, direction, and timing to overcome various forms of target challenges.

The game environments are exquisitely detailed, from tranquil forests to stormy mountaintops, with each scene adding different atmospheres and challenges to the archery experience. As players progress through the game, they will unlock more advanced arrow types and skills, facing increasingly complex targets and environmental conditions. Feeling Arrow is suitable for all types of players - whether you're a casual player seeking relaxation or a challenger pursuing perfect technique, you'll find enjoyment in this game.

How to Play

  1. Select a Bow: Choose a bow type suitable for the current challenge from available options. Each bow has different range and stability characteristics.
  2. Aim: Use the mouse or touchscreen to move the crosshair and aim at your target. Consider the effects of distance and wind direction.
  3. Draw the Bow: Hold the left mouse button or press the touchscreen to draw the bowstring. Watch the power indicator to control your shooting power.
  4. Maintain Stability: Keep steady while in the drawn position; excessive shaking will reduce accuracy. In some situations, you can hold specific keys to control breathing.
  5. Release the Arrow: Release the left mouse button or your finger to fire the arrow. Observe the arrow's flight trajectory to adjust for your next shot.

Tips for Victory

  • → Use the practice mode to familiarize yourself with different bow characteristics and feel before taking on real challenges. Learn the advantages and disadvantages of each bow type.
  • → Pay attention to the wind direction indicators in the environment. In windy conditions, you'll need to adjust your aim according to wind strength and direction.
  • → For long-distance targets, consider gravity's effect. Your aim point should be slightly higher than the actual target; the greater the distance, the higher you need to aim.
  • → Master breath control techniques by holding specific keys (usually the Shift key) during critical shooting moments to slow crosshair movement and improve precision.
  • → For moving targets, you need to predict their position. Aim where the target will be, not where it currently is. This requires accurate perception of target speed.
